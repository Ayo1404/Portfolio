Projects & Experience

Wind Tunnel Validation

Designed a small-scale wind tunnel to validate CFD simulations of conceptual car parts for aerodynamic analysis.

This project involved creating physical models to compare against CFD results, allowing me to learn about flow visualization, pressure distribution, and experimental testing methods commonly used in motorsport engineering.

Electrically Assisted Pedal Cycle Gearbox – Second Year

Developed a two-stage gearbox to transmit power from an electric motor to the rear wheel. Achieved a 2:1 result in the project.

Calculated required gear ratios to meet motor output while complying with UK regulations, explored multiple concepts, and selected a compact two-stage gearbox. Produced detailed CAD models, ran FEA simulations, and prototyped the system. Iterative testing revealed a gear failure, which led to a redesign using clamped and bolted joints. The final system was robust and functional, enhancing my skills in CAD, simulation, design iteration, and prototyping under real-world constraints.

Water Bottle Rocket Launcher – First Year

Designed and built a compressed air-powered water bottle rocket launcher with safety mechanisms, pressure regulation, and trajectory evaluation.

Manufactured the mechanical launcher using 3D printing, CNC machining, and workshop tools. Used MATLAB to simulate trajectories, iterated launcher design for stability, and refined rockets with fins and nose cones. On test day, all rockets landed within 10 m of the target, earning a first-class result. Developed skills in CAD, MATLAB programming, data analysis, prototyping, and experimental validation.
